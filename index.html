🌡 Temperature

Sensor Specifications
HTS221 — Capacitive Digital Sensor
Measurement Range−40 °C to +120 °C
Optimal Accuracy Range15 °C to 40 °C
Accuracy± 0.5 °C (15–40 °C)
Resolution0.016 °C
Output Data Rate1 / 7 / 12.5 Hz
Supply Voltage1.7 V – 3.6 V
⚠ Known Limitations
  • CPU heat transfer from the Pi causes readings to be 1–3 °C above true ambient temperature.
  • Accuracy degrades significantly outside the 15–40 °C optimal band.
  • Requires calibration offset for outdoor deployments.
  • Not suitable for sub-zero precision measurements without compensation.

💧 Humidity

Sensor Specifications
HTS221 — Capacitive Digital Sensor
Measurement Range0 – 100 % rH
Optimal Accuracy Range20 – 80 % rH
Accuracy± 3.5 % rH (20–80 % rH)
Resolution0.004 % rH
Response Time< 4 seconds (63 % step)
Hysteresis± 1 % rH
⚠ Known Limitations
  • Shares the same die as the temperature sensor — board heat affects humidity readings (lower than actual).
  • Accuracy drops sharply below 20 % rH and above 80 % rH.
  • Long-term drift can occur without periodic recalibration.
  • Condensation on the sensor can cause temporary erratic readings.

IR Blocking FilterIntegrated
Integration Time2.4 ms – 614 ms (configurable)
InterfaceI²C (400 kHz)
Supply Voltage2.7 V – 3.6 V
⚠ Known Limitations
  • Sensor is mounted under the LED matrix — readings can be attenuated if LEDs are active.
  • Only available on Sense HAT V2; V1 boards lack a light sensor entirely.
  • Spectral response does not perfectly match the human eye (CIE luminosity curve).
  • Enclosure or HAT orientation can block light path, requiring mounting considerations.
